Here is a comprehensive, expansive plan to build Justin’s "Open-Source Consulting" website. It’s designed to be plain, steady, and neighborly, yet undeniably authoritative.

---

## 1. The Core Brand & Ethos
**The Vibe:** The Anti-Consultant. No corporate jargon, no fluff. Just cold, hard numbers presented with the warmth of a trusted mentor.
**The Hook:** "I’ll give you the exact tools to solve your problem for free. If you want me to run the numbers for you, you hire me."

## 2. Site Architecture & Navigation
The site should be incredibly clean, driving people immediately to his frameworks.

* **Home:** The Philosophy & The Guarantee.
* **"Spreadsheet Says..." (The Lab):** Interactive, free financial models.
* **Open-Source Audits:** Downloadable 30-point performance checklists.
* **Consulting Services:** How to hire Justin (The paid tier).
* **About Justin:** The 30-year pedigree and La Trobe history.

---

## 3. Page-by-Page Breakdown

### Home Page
* **Headline:** Clarity through Numbers.
* **Sub-headline:** 30 years of accounting and strategy, distilled into free tools you can use right now.
* **The Proposition:** A clear, working-class statement. *“Decisions shouldn’t be made on gut feelings or boardroom politics. They should be made on facts. Here are my models. Use them. If you need an expert to guide you through them, let's talk.”*
* **The Guarantee (Front and Center):** Outline his value-based pricing immediately. *“The $10k Promise: If my costing review doesn’t identify at least $10,000 in savings or value, you don’t pay.”*

### The "Spreadsheet Says" Section (The Lead Magnet)
This is the most brilliant part of his strategy. It gamifies finance. It should have simple, 3-line models with 1-page assumptions.

* **The Everyday Decisions (To build trust & traffic):**
    * *The Caravan vs. The Sedan & Motel:* A lifetime cost analysis of buying a caravan versus staying in hotels.
    * *The Backyard Pool:* Does it add value, or is it a 10-year money pit?
* **The Boardroom Decisions (To capture corporate leads):**
    * *In-House vs. Outsourced IT/Finance:* A model to calculate the true cost of an internal department versus outsourcing.
    * *The Hardware Lifecycle:* The math behind holding onto equipment versus upgrading.
* **The Property Matrix Tool:** A downloadable framework dividing real estate into the A/B/C/D categories.
    * **Category A:** The sacred cows (Hold).
    * **Categories B, C, D:** Deal with them via the spreadsheet (Run the rate of return, capital growth, and the 20x rent rule to trigger an automatic Buy/Sell/Keep decision).

### Open-Source Audits & Reviews
Justin mentioned wanting to get an auditing license and doing performance audits. He should list the exact programmatic steps online.
* **The 30-Point Checklists:** Give away the exact steps he takes to check bank statements, verify aging, and audit performance. "Here is my playbook. Do it yourself, or hire me to execute it objectively."
* **Post-Implementation Reviews (The Autopsy):** A section dedicated to reviewing past government or corporate projects (looking back 5-10 years) to answer: *Was this actually worth it?* This appeals massively to local councils and boards who want to avoid past mistakes.

### Strategy & Executive Counsel
This is where Justin's "left field" brilliance shines. He isn't just a bean counter; he’s an executive coach backed by math.
* **Strategy Sessions:** Offer days dedicated to "Successes and Failures in Economic History."
* **The Toolkit:** Highlight his use of simple, effective tools:
    * *Force Field Analysis* (Equilibrium, opposing vs. restraining forces).
    * *The 80/20 Rule* (Stop wasting time on the noise).
* **Executive Decision Help:** A specialized, highly confidential service for CEOs and Boards. *Stay or Leave? Hire or Fire?* Taking the emotion out of the hardest decisions a leader has to make.

Given your own toolkit, you can build this rapidly and beautifully.
